A Universal Conversation Assistant

An AI-powered copilot to provide real-time guidance, steering, and analysis in any conversation, from professional negotiations to personal discussions.

Listen & Read

With permission, the assistant ingests conversations from voice calls, chats, and emails to build a complete picture.

Track & Understand

Continuously tracks context, intent, tone, and emotional state to understand the dynamics of the dialogue.

Guide & Suggest

Allows users to prompt on-demand for guidance, such as "Suggest a professional but firm reply."

Alert & Notify

Generates real-time alerts for negative sentiment shifts, topic drift, or manipulative language.

Stage 1: Input Capture

This stage focuses on ingesting data from all relevant sources. It uses official APIs for reliability and has robust mechanisms for handling various media types to ensure no part of the conversation is lost.

  • Chat/Text: Connects to WhatsApp Business and Slack APIs for real-time message ingestion.
  • Media: Captures images, voice notes, and documents shared within the conversation.

Stage 2: Preprocessing

Raw data is cleaned and structured for analysis. This step is crucial for accuracy, as it standardizes the input and extracts key metadata that informs the system's understanding of context and time.

  • Text Normalization: Cleans text by correcting typos and standardizing slang or abbreviations.
  • Media Analysis: Transcribes audio, extracts text from images (OCR), and generates captions.
  • Temporal Tagging: Each piece of data is timestamped to maintain conversational order and analyze time gaps.

Stage 3: Central Mental Model

The processed data feeds into the core of the system: a long-term, evolving model of the conversation and its participants. This is where deep context is stored and interconnected.

  • Evolving Context: Continuously updates its understanding with each new message or interaction.
  • Vector Database: Stores text and media embeddings for fast, semantic searching of conversational memory.

Stage 4: Context Filtering

Not all conversation is equally important. This stage intelligently separates relevant, goal-oriented discussions from unrelated small talk or off-topic threads, ensuring the AI's suggestions are focused and pertinent.

  • Thread Clustering: Groups messages into distinct topics or threads.
  • Relevance Scoring: Prioritizes active, important threads and applies a decay function to older, less relevant context.

Stage 5: AI Engine

The filtered, relevant context is passed to the AI engine, which generates real-time assistance. This is the "brains" of the operation, where intelligent insights and suggestions are formulated.

  • Steerability & Alerts: Generates suggestions to guide the conversation and flags potential issues.
  • Style Adaptation: Adapts its suggestions to match the user's natural communication style.
  • Persona Integration: Incorporates knowledge of the user's and others' personalities for tailored advice.

Stage 6: Output

The final stage delivers the AI's assistance to the user through a clean, intuitive interface. The goal is to provide helpful guidance without being intrusive.

  • Suggestions & Rephrasing: Offers alternative ways to phrase messages to achieve a specific tone or outcome.
  • Reminders: Brings up unresolved points or past commitments from the conversation history.
  • Proactive Nudges: Suggests conversation starters or follow-ups based on temporal context.

The Core: Central Mental Model Formation

At the heart of the assistant is the 'Mental Model,' an evolving, long-term understanding of the conversation's context, participants, and history. It's built from multiple layers of data to provide highly personalized and contextually aware suggestions.

Building Blocks of Understanding

Historical Conversations: Learns from past interactions across all connected platforms.
Inferred Personality Traits: Analyzes talking style, tone, and preferences to model participants.
Temporal Awareness: Tracks time gaps and the evolving nature of relationships over months or years.
External References: Notes mentions of third parties (friends, colleagues) to build a richer social context.
Media Enrichment: Integrates context from images, voice notes, and documents, including their metadata.

Practical Application

This deep model allows for nuanced, intelligent suggestions:

  • →

    If you haven't talked in months, the assistant suggests a warm reintroduction instead of a direct question.

  • →

    If a random off-topic chat appears, it is ignored unless explicitly linked back to a relevant, ongoing thread.

  • →

    If a third person is discussed often, the assistant builds a lightweight model of them for richer future reference.

Feasibility & Technology

This section assesses the project's technical viability, outlining solutions to key challenges and the specific technologies required for implementation. Use the tabs below to explore the analysis.

Handling Key Challenges

While ambitious, the project's primary challenges have viable solutions using modern techniques.

Unrelated Chats & Noise

Use topic modeling and clustering algorithms to isolate and prioritize relevant conversation threads.

Time Gaps & Stale Context

Implement decay functions so older context becomes less influential, while key metadata (e.g., last contact date) remains.

Media & Multimodal Handling

Transcribe audio and analyze images to extract text and captions, enriching context instead of ignoring media.

Privacy & Security

Store conversation embeddings securely and provide users with granular control to pause or reset the mental model.

NLP & AI Models

LLMs like Nemotron, GPT, or LLaMA for generation and steering. Rasa can be used for more structured intent classification.

Vector Databases

Pinecone, Weaviate, or FAISS for storing and searching the mental model's embeddings efficiently.

Backend & Real-time

Node.js with Express for the server, and WebSockets for low-latency, bidirectional communication.

APIs & Integration

Official WhatsApp Business API and Slack API for reliable data ingestion from chat platforms.

Media Analysis

Multimodal models like OpenAI's CLIP (for image understanding) and Whisper (for audio transcription) to process non-text data.

Phase 1: Text-First Prototype

The initial focus is on creating a browser extension for Chrome/Edge that works with popular web-based chat platforms. This allows for rapid development and testing of the core AI features without the complexity of real-time voice processing.

  • Target Platform: WhatsApp Web.
  • Core Functionality: Read the conversation, summarize the other person's intent, and suggest 1-2 relevant reply options.
  • Initial Alert: Simple alert if the tone of the conversation shifts significantly toward negative sentiment.

Phase 2: Add Voice Support

This phase tackles the challenge of latency by integrating a real-time speech-to-text engine. The initial targets are platforms with more accessible APIs for audio streaming, like video conferencing tools.

  • Target Platforms: Zoom and Google Meet.
  • Core Functionality: Use Whisper to transcribe live calls and display real-time steering suggestions and insights in a side panel.
  • User Interface: A non-intrusive overlay that doesn't obstruct the main video call window.

Phase 3: Build Alert System

With the core text and voice features in place, this phase focuses on developing the sophisticated alert engine to provide proactive warnings about conversational risks.

  • Detection Capabilities: Identify emotional shifts (e.g., from neutral to defensive), contradictions in statements, and potentially manipulative patterns.
  • Alert Delivery: Lightweight, subtle popups with clear and concise warnings (e.g., "Caution: Tone has shifted to defensive").

Phase 4: General-Purpose Expansion

The final phase of the MVP roadmap is about expanding the tool's reach and adding user customization features, transforming it from a prototype into a versatile, general-purpose assistant.

  • Platform Expansion: Create plugins for Slack, Gmail, and LinkedIn messages.
  • Custom Goals: Allow users to define specific objectives for a conversation, such as "steer toward closing the deal" or "keep the conversation casual and friendly."
